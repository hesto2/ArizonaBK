Let Us Help You Rebuild Your Life Through Bankruptcy

If you're drowning in debt, you may feel as though there's no way out. The light at the end of the tunnel is hard to see, but we're here to show you that it is there and it's closer than you think. Filing for bankruptcy might just be the choice that helps you overcome your financial struggles and finally achieve stability. Bankruptcy is often wrongly associated with failure, but, in reality, it is an excellent way to gain financial freedom.

If you're considering bankruptcy, the first step is determining which kind of bankruptcy is right for you. Chapter 7 bankruptcy requires that you liquidate your nonexempt property. The proceeds from that liquidation process will go toward paying back your creditors. To qualify for Chapter 7 bankruptcy, you typically must have a low income and the inability to commit to a repayment plan. The efficiency of a Chapter 7 filing often makes it an excellent choice for individuals looking to move forward as soon as possible.

Conversely, Chapter 13 bankruptcy is a good fit for individuals with a stable income who are able to agree to a repayment plan and want to avoid liquidating their assets. Typically, your repayment plan will last anywhere from three to five years, making this process a much more time-consuming one.
